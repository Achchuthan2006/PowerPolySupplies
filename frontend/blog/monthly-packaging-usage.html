How much packaging do dry cleaners use per month?

This planner helps you estimate monthly usage for garment bags and related packaging so you can restock on a schedule (and avoid running out mid-week).

Simple formula
Monthly garments = (garments/day) × (operating days/month), then add a 5–12% buffer.

Step 1: Estimate garments per month

Example: 160 garments/day × 26 days = 4,160 garments/month

Step 2: Split by garment type (your “mix”)

Most shops have a predictable mix. Start with this and adjust over time:

Category Typical share What you need
Shirts / tops 40–50% Short garment bags
Pants / skirts 20–30% Short garment bags (often)
Suits / dresses 15–25% Medium/long garment bags
Coats / outerwear 5–15% Long + wider (often gusseted) bags

Step 3: Add a buffer (important)

  • 5–8% buffer for pickup-heavy shops with gentle handling.
  • 8–12% buffer for delivery routes (more movement, rewraps, tears).
  • Increase buffer during peak seasons (winter coats, wedding season).

Step 4: Build a restock plan

  • Staples (top 1–2 sizes): keep 2–4 weeks on-hand.
  • Specialty (extra-long / gusseted): keep 4–8 weeks on-hand.
  • Review monthly: if you’re consistently finishing early, increase the reorder quantity.

Step 5: Don’t forget thickness

If your buffer is high because of tearing, you may save money by moving to thicker film.
