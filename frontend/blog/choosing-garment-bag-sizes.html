Guide to choosing garment bag sizes

Picking the right size reduces wrinkles, prevents torn bags, and makes customers happy. Use this guide to choose the best width, length, and style for your daily volume.

Fast rule
Bag width ≈ garment width + 4–6" and bag length ≈ garment length + 4–8".

1) Measure the garment (not the hanger)

  • Width: shoulder-to-shoulder at the widest point.
  • Length: from the top of the hanger hook area to the bottom of the garment.
  • Bulk: coats and layered items need extra room (gusset / wider bag).

2) Choose width first (then length)

  • If width is too small, bags tear and shoulders get crushed.
  • If width is too big, it wastes film and looks sloppy (but it still “works”).
  • If you’re between sizes, choose the wider option for coats and multi-items on one hanger.

3) Common use-cases (quick reference)

Use-case Typical width Typical length Notes
Shirts / blouses 18–21" 36–40" High daily volume for most cleaners.
Pants (folded) 18–21" 36–40" Fold on hanger; shorter bags often work.
Suits / dresses 21–24" 40–54" Go longer for formalwear and maxi dresses.
Coats / outerwear 24–28" 48–60" Consider gusset + thicker film for transport.
Wedding / formal gowns 24–30"+ 60–72" Extra room reduces snagging and wrinkling.

4) When to choose gusseted bags

  • Bulky winter coats, layered suits, comforters/blankets, or multiple garments on one hanger.
  • Delivery routes (extra space reduces pressure points and tearing).
  • If you often re-bag items after noticing crushed shoulders.

5) Thickness and handling

If customers carry garments long distance, or you’re packaging heavier items, a thicker film can reduce tears and rewraps.
