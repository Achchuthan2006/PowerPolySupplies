Heavy vs Extra Heavy: which thickness do you need?

“Heavy” and “extra heavy” are common labels, but what matters is performance: tear resistance, puncture resistance, and how your customers handle the package.

Quick decision
If you’re re-bagging often or getting torn bags in delivery, move up to a thicker film once—usually cheaper than double-bagging.

What these labels usually mean

  • Heavy: great everyday choice for most packaging and garment protection.
  • Extra Heavy: better for sharp corners, heavy loads, rough handling, or longer delivery routes.

Thickness naming varies by supplier; if you need a precise spec, check the product’s stated mil/gauge.

Choose “Heavy” when

  • Items are lightweight and smooth (folded garments, liners, standard packaging).
  • Most orders are local and handled gently.
  • You want best value for high monthly volume.

Choose “Extra Heavy” when

  • Items have edges/corners (bulk hangers, boxed accessories, hardware).
  • Loads are heavy or stacked in transit.
  • You want fewer tears, fewer returns, and fewer rewraps.

Real-world examples

Scenario Recommended Why
Front-counter pickup, short drives Heavy Good durability at the best cost.
Delivery route with multiple stops Extra Heavy Less tearing from movement + stacking.
Bulk hangers in a bag Extra Heavy Prevents punctures from hook/edges.
Low-volume specialty packaging Extra Heavy Fewer mistakes/rewraps is worth it.
